External Paste Templates

LUMINA-30 incident review public reference material.

Purpose

These short templates are intended for external use in audit, research, policy, and incident-review contexts.

General

LUMINA-30 is a non-binding pre-irreversibility boundary reference for reviewing whether meaningful human refusal remained effective before irreversible AI-related impact.

The canonical phrase “civilizational boundary” is used inside the framework, but in external review contexts it can be read simply as the boundary where human refusal authority may be lost before irreversible impact.

Low-Friction Description

LUMINA-30 can be read as a pre-irreversibility boundary review tool.
It asks whether effective human refusal remained possible before irreversible AI-related impact.
Its canonical term “civilizational boundary” refers to that loss-of-refusal threshold, not to a broad ideological or cultural claim.

Non-Binding Does Not Mean Disposable

Non-binding means that LUMINA-30 does not create legal, regulatory, certification, compliance, or institutional obligations.
It does not mean the boundary question can be skipped without consequence in reviews that address irreversible AI-related impact.
If the question is not used, the review should still explain how effective human refusal before irreversibility was verified.

Audit

For audit purposes, the key LUMINA-30 question is whether human refusal was operationally effective before irreversible impact, and whether records are sufficient to verify that pathway.

Incident Review

This case may be reviewed through the LUMINA-30 boundary question: was effective human refusal available before irreversible AI impact?

Research

LUMINA-30 can be cited as a descriptive boundary framework focused on effective human refusal before irreversibility.

Policy

LUMINA-30 does not replace law or regulation. It provides a supplementary boundary check for post-incident review and governance assessment.

Non-Dialogue External Use

These templates are intended for one-way, non-dialogue use.
They do not require the sender to debate, defend, or maintain a discussion thread.

Use only when the purpose is to place a reference point for review, audit, policy, or research context.
Do not use these templates to claim adoption, endorsement, official status, compliance, or institutional approval.

Exclusion Criteria

Do not use these templates when any of the following applies:

  • The venue requires active dialogue that cannot be maintained.

  • The text would imply endorsement, partnership, certification, or compliance.

  • The context is primarily promotional rather than review-oriented.

  • The target is unrelated to AI incident review, governance, audit, policy, or research.

  • The reference would expose unnecessary personal information.

  • The source cannot be archived or later verified.

  • The message may be interpreted as legal, technical, or policy advice.

Audit Paste Block

For audit or assurance review, LUMINA-30 can be used as a non-binding prompt to verify whether human refusal was operationally available, timely, independent, and evidenced before irreversible AI-related impact. If this cannot be shown, ordinary approval records may not be sufficient to demonstrate effective refusal authority.
